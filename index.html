History of Côte d'Ivoire

In 1687 the French established a misson at Assinie. They wanted ivory and other goods from the west coast of Africa. Along with the Pepper or Grain Coast (Liberia), a Gold Coast (Ghana), and a Slave Coast (Togo, Benin and Nigeria), Côte d'Ivoire formed a group of areas which supplied goods to Europeans, mostly Portuguese and French merchants. The French weren't a major power in the region until the mid-nineteenth century, when French Admiral Louis Édouard Bouët-Willaumez signed treaties with kingdoms in the area to make it a French protectorate. At the end of the 1880s, French control over the coastal regions was established. On the 7th of August 1960 Côte d'Ivoire achieved independence after roughly seventy years of French control.

À la fin des années 1880, le contrôle français sur les régions côtières est établi. Le 7 août 1960, la Côte d'Ivoire accède à son indépendance après environ soixante-dix ans de contrôle français.
